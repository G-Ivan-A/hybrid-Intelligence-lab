# EU AI Act Risk Tiering

## Source, author, link

- Source: EU AI Act materials from the European Commission.
- Organization: European Commission.
- Link: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai

## Practice

Before importing or shipping an AI workflow, assign a risk tier and document why
that tier is plausible for the current use case.

## Problem

Teams can treat all AI use as equal. The EU AI Act model is useful even outside
EU compliance because it forces a context-of-use question before deployment.

## Apply In Hub

1. Classify the workflow as prohibited/unacceptable, high risk, limited risk, or
   minimal/no risk for the project context.
2. If the workflow touches employment, education, identity, safety, rights, or
   public-facing decisions, escalate for human review.
3. Record the tier in the issue or local project governance artifact.
4. Keep the Hub recommendation separate from legal compliance decisions.

## Output

A short risk tier note that can block, approve, or scope down a proposed AI
practice.

## Limits

This node is not a legal interpretation of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. It is an
operational triage practice.
